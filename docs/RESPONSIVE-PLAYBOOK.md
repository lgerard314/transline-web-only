# Responsive translation playbook — get it right on the first pass

**Read this in full before optimizing ANY designed section for non-desktop viewports.** It was distilled from the 2026-06 miller-web home responsive pass, where nearly every round of rework traced to one of a small set of repeatable process failures — all of them preventable by following this doc. The verification protocol lives in the root `CLAUDE.md` ("How to verify responsive work") and is mandatory; this doc covers the part that comes BEFORE verification: how to design the translation correctly the first time, how to specify motion, and the implementation recipes that are now house patterns (each with a worked in-repo reference).

The failure tax this doc exists to prevent, by actual count from that session: the VBEC section was rebuilt three times because the first attempt re-skinned an inherited mobile layout instead of translating the desktop design; the Lifetime section's motion took three rounds because a token "background zoom" was substituted for the desktop's expanding-panel entrance; the capability rail's timing was redone twice because "in position by X" was read as "allowed to finish any time before X"; and one shipped-broken phone layout came from deleting CSS without checking what it was neutralizing.

## 0. The standing contract (applies to every section, both apps)

- **Desktop IS the design.** logan designs on desktop; the desktop render is the brief. Desktop must not change at all during responsive work — every edit sits behind width/orientation/pointer gates, and desktop byte-invariance is re-verified every round.
- **The bar, per section per viewport:** "is this an exceptional, high-end, expensive-looking website that is potentially award-winning?" Not "does it fit."
- **Contextually the best design, not generically the flashiest (logan 2026-06-11).** This is a hazardous-waste management company serving industrial generators, public agencies, small businesses, and residents — not a startup landing page. Per page, ask WHO lands here and WHAT they need (a dispatcher booking a pickup reads differently than a brand statement): stand out and be unique, but never stray from what the company does or forget who it serves. A grand marketing treatment on a utilitarian page is a bar FAILURE even if it's beautiful.
- **Surfaces:** desktop / small desktop–big tablet / tablet / phone (~460 nominal, flexible per component) — crossed with orientation and pointer type. Portrait is the dominant real-world orientation on tablets and phones; design portrait first among the small surfaces.
- **Pins on portrait are decided by the MOTION TEST, not banned outright (logan 2026-06-11).** A pin that exists to hold an oversized STATIC composition (who-we-serve's hover stage, lifetime's full-height band, VBEC's reveal runway) never survives on portrait — those un-pin and their motion becomes entry scrubs (§4.1). But a choreography whose mechanism IS the hold — "wait until the section fills the screen, then zoom into the screen" (the careers dive) — **pins on every surface including portrait**; un-pinning it breaks the design rather than adapting it. Only the home hero (and such full-screen-converging pins while pinned) is ever 100vh on portrait.
- **Motion is scroll-tied, not autonomous.** The house language is scroll-scrubbed choreography; the only "live" animations are small one-shot reveals. Horizontal thumb-scrollable rails/strips are welcome on small screens.
- **The home page is the motion showcase; other pages get a lighter budget (logan 2026-06-11).** Only the home hero — and a full-screen-converging pin while pinned (previous bullet) — is ever full-screen. Interior/other pages should carry SOME scroll-tied life when they're built or converted — a couple of well-chosen moments in the home vocabulary — but never the home page's density of pinned/scrubbed sections.

## 1. Phase one — the desktop-intent inventory (mandatory, before any edit)

Render the section on desktop (the live page, not the code) and write down, explicitly:

1. **Every component and what it is FOR** — eyebrow, title, lead, CTAs, each media element, figures, ornaments, frames/containers, background graphics. A white card around photos is chrome with a job; a background map is a stage; a sliver gallery is a curated one-open reveal. If you can't say what a component is for, you can't translate it.
2. **Every motion beat, in order, with its trigger and what it COMMUNICATES.** The Lifetime panel growing from a small opaque-map box into the full band is not decoration — it IS the section's identity, and "add back the motion" meant porting THAT, not adding a generic zoom. Write the desktop choreography as a sequence ("intro slides down → photo column rises → highlights grow → media swipes right → diamonds roll in") so each beat can be deliberately kept, re-staged, or consciously dropped per surface.
3. **Every interaction state** — hover, active, selected, expanded — what it reveals, and whether the content it reveals is reachable without a fine pointer.
4. **The props/config the section is actually MOUNTED with** (the `page.jsx` composition + content module) — behavior assumptions die here: the home careers section passes `autoScroll: false`, so the "auto-advancing" dive is actually pure scroll-scrub on the live page. Read the call site, not just the component.

Two scope rules while planning:

- **A specific idea from logan inside an "optimize the section" ask is an INPUT, not the scope.** He hands you one improvement ("add a view-more"), but the deliverable is still the full composition pass — order, widths/bleeds, every component's presentation, type scale, motion, AND his idea. Applying only the idea reads as not having looked at the section (it happened; he called it out).
- **His geometry words are contracts.** "Centered", "by the time X passes the bottom", "full screen" — implement THAT word's geometry, not a neighboring anchor that's easier to build. "Fully on screen" was shipped for "centered" and bounced.

Then write the **translation plan per surface** before touching code: for each inventory item, decide keep / re-stage / re-orient / replace — and say where its motion goes. Two hard rules here:

- **Never accept an existing smaller-viewport layout as the brief.** It is usually an artifact of an earlier pass, not a design. Optimizing the VBEC mobile photo wall (instead of translating the desktop's one-open hero + sliver gallery) was this session's worst failure.
- **Content behind hover must survive on touch as content, not disappear.** Hover-revealed detail becomes tap-to-select with an explicit way OFF (outside-tap clear); grayscale-until-hover photo treatments become full-color at rest on touch (otherwise the un-hoverable surface is stranded permanently in the "waiting" state).

## 2. Phase two — the design vocabulary (approved moves; reach for these first)

These are patterns logan asked for or explicitly approved this pass. They are the house starting points for narrow-surface translation, not an exhaustive limit.

- **Horizontal snap rails / film strips** for photo sets and card sets: ~62–84vw cards, scroll-snap, hidden scrollbar, the **next card partially peeking** as the swipe affordance. No stacked-image walls, ever, on any page — a wall of full-width images with no purpose other than being images is banned.
- **Edge bleeds are directional and meaningful.** On the home VBEC, photos bleed off the RIGHT screen edge and the capability rail bleeds off the LEFT — mirrored full-bleeds bookend the column and read as deliberate. A bleed edge and a peek edge are different jobs: the bleed signals "this strip extends beyond the page," the peek signals "swipe me." Don't put both jobs on the same edge by default.
- **A scroller's rest pose and reading direction are part of the spec.** Ask (or infer from the bleed direction): which item is fully visible at rest, at which edge, and which way does the thumb travel through the sequence? (The capability rail rests with card #1 flush at the content-right edge, #7 furthest off the left bleed, and a left-to-right swipe travels 1→7 — built with `row-reverse`, see §4.6.)
- **White media cards** (the desktop card chrome) survive on phones: warm surface + hairline + shadow, the card's leading edge extending slightly into the gutter while the first photo's edge stays exactly on the content line, the frame consistent on three sides and **absent on the bleeding side until the last item is reached** (end padding seats the final photo with the same inset).
- **Adjacent sections must not repeat a motif.** A vertical capability spine directly above the history section's vertical timeline spine was rejected purely for that adjacency — check the neighbors before committing a treatment.
- **Legibility beats geometry.** When a desktop graphic taxes its content at column width (capability names wrapping 4 lines at 11px inside a 100px rhombus; detail copy behind an undiscoverable tap), re-stage the CONTENT comfortably (cards, rails, rows) and keep the graphic's DNA — same tones, numbering, diamond marks — rather than shrinking the graphic until the content suffocates.
- **Staggered/zigzag label rows** buy type size: alternating items drop ~half an element height so neighbors clear each other's baseline and the type can grow 20–30%.
- **Type on portrait is bigger than you think.** Display titles ≈34–50px at 390–720 and wrapping to 3–4 lines is welcome; one-column card titles ≈30px (a 24px title that "passed" was later bumped +25% on request); body ≥14px; mono labels/details ≥11–12px floors. When torn between two sizes, take the larger — every type-size correction this session was "bigger."
- **Spacing beats compression.** Each visual beat of a section (eyebrow / media / labels / heading) needs a real pause — 6–16px gaps between beats read as "compressed" and were called out. And in flow (un-pinned) mode, a panel edge and the content start often coincide by construction; flowing surfaces need explicit in-panel insets (≈34–50px top, ≈40–80px bottom) — see §4.7.
- **Eyebrows open their band CENTERED ABOVE the content, even on thin full-bleed strips.** The affiliates marquee's inline-left label read as "misplaced" and ate the track's width (cramping phone logos); recomposing it as the house section-opener — diamond + mono 12px centered above a full-width strip — fixed both. Eyebrow size is a fixed 12px everywhere; a viewport clamp on one eyebrow drifted to 14px and read oversized.
- **Faint tiled brand-graphic washes** are approved background vocabulary (the final-CTA dumptruck weave): the section's own SVG art tiled on a ±30°-ish diagonal, alternating row direction, brick-offset rows, at ~half the source stage's prominence — optionally with opposing scroll-parallax "lanes" so each motif drifts toward its own heading (§4.15/§4.16 for the implementation).

## 3. Phase three — motion contracts (write them before coding)

For every motion on a flow surface, write a one-line contract: **progress source → start anchor → end anchor**, where both anchors are **exact geometric events**, not tuned thresholds.

- **Derive the WHOLE mechanism from the DESKTOP choreography's spatial contract — where the motion starts relative to the viewport and where its focal point lives (logan, 2026-06-11).** Two worked cases: the careers dive begins at the pin moment (the stage already fills the screen, the centre photo at the screen's centre) and zooms toward that centred focal point until the final frame IS the screen — a full-screen-converging effect whose pinned hold IS the mechanism, so it **keeps its pin on every surface, portrait included** ("wait until the section fills the screen, then zoom into the screen"; the compact surfaces only re-compose the mosaic and shorten the dive). An un-pinned translation of such an effect is structurally wrong no matter how the anchors are tuned — it was tried twice here (entry-anchored, then centred-window) and both failed the intent. The lifetime panel, by contrast, is edge-born and non-centred — its growth visibly starts while the section is still arriving — so the un-pinned entry scrub ("lands when fully visible") is correct THERE. Don't default to one pattern: read the desktop and give the small-viewport version the same spatial relationship to the viewport, up to and including the pin itself.

- **Anchor vocabulary that shipped** (all as `p = clamp01(...)` formulas in a writer, §4.2): "p hits 1 exactly when the section bottom meets the viewport bottom" (`(vh − top) / (secBottom − top)`); "completes when the title's top reaches the viewport bottom"; "completes when the band is fully on-screen"; "starts when the top half of the start-pose box is visible."
- **"In position by the time X passes the bottom of the screen" means: lands AT that exact frame, with assembly VISIBLY IN PROGRESS the whole approach.** Finishing early is as wrong as finishing late — spread per-item slices across the full runway so the last item settles at p=1.000, not at p=0.6. This was corrected twice; treat it as the default reading of any "by the time" spec.
- **Derive ordering guarantees from geometry, not tuning.** "The figures band must finish before the rail below starts" is guaranteed forever by "band completes when fully visible" (it sits above the rail), and can never drift the way tuned thresholds do when spacing changes.
- **Un-pinning is not de-animating.** Every desktop choreography beat survives on flow surfaces as a scroll scrub (reversible both directions) unless the translation plan consciously drops it. Port the actual desktop move — same variables and formulas where possible (the ≤900 diamond roll-in reuses the pinned sequence's exact `--cap-tx/-ty/-rot/-sc` math) — never a token substitute.
- **Perceptible or it doesn't exist.** An effect whose CSS variables provably change but which is invisible at the position the user actually looks (a 7%-opacity wash being zoomed; a chain-draw completing below the fold within the first 100px of entry) counts as MISSING. Effects need their own visibility arc (e.g. enter at 0.2 opacity, breathe down to the settled wash) and their completion staged where the eye is. Verify motion BY EYE at mid-scroll frames, not only by variable probes.
- **A pin FREEZES geometry-driven scrubs — so any scrub inside a pinned section must complete AT or BEFORE the pin frame.** Sticky engagement stops the section's rects from moving; whatever pose a geometry-anchored scrub holds at that frame is frozen for the entire hold (the who-we-serve roll-in froze mid-spin — crooked diamonds — on wide/short screens). On pin-capable surfaces, anchor such scrubs to the pin frame itself (`progress = 1 − sectionTop/runup`, hitting exactly 1 at sticky engagement) so the end of the motion IS the pin point; flow surfaces keep their own anchors. Worked reference: `sector-grid-motion-03.jsx` pinMode branch.
- **Reveals follow VISUAL order** (position-sorted, §4.5), everything is reversible with scroll, and `prefers-reduced-motion` rests every surface fully settled (never un-entered).

## 4. Implementation recipes (house patterns, with worked references)

### 4.1 Landscape-only pin gate + JS mirror (static-composition pins)
For pins that hold an oversized STATIC composition (the §0 motion test says these never survive portrait): CSS `@media (min-width: <N>px) and (orientation: landscape) and (min-height: <M>px) and (prefers-reduced-motion: no-preference)` wraps EVERY pin rule. JS: a `canPin()` built from `matchMedia` mirrors of the exact same queries gates the pin effect, the track-height writer, and any pin-state classes — so JS state can never fire without the CSS pin. These never pin on portrait at any width or pointer type (logan tests fine-pointer portrait windows via devtools responsive mode, so `pointer: coarse` exclusions are NOT sufficient). Worked references: `home/lifetime.css:90` + `lifetime-reel-01.jsx`, `home/sectors.css:653` + `sector-diamonds-04.jsx`, `home/facility-pin.css:14` + `media-split-01.jsx` (`pinCapable`). Full-screen-converging choreographies are the exception and pin everywhere (§0/§3) — there the media gate selects LAYOUT only (worked reference: `zoom-collage-01.jsx` `PIN_MQS` choosing wide vs compact mosaic, `home/careers.css` compact block).

### 4.2 Flow writer → CSS-var slices (the scrub backbone)
One rAF-coalesced passive scroll listener per section (gated to the pin's exact complement, skipped under reduced motion) writes per-section progress vars (`--capalt-p`, `--figs-p`, `--fac2-strip-p`, `--lr-*`, …) from rect-anchored formulas. CSS consumes them per item: `--t: clamp(0, calc((var(--p, 1) - var(--thr)) * rate), 1)` with `--thr` derived from the item index, then `opacity: var(--t); transform: …calc((1 - var(--t)) * <distance>)…`. Defaults (`var(--p, 1)`) rest everything settled for no-JS and reduced motion. Worked reference: the flow writer in `media-split-01.jsx` + the `.mw-capalt__item` / `.mw-fac2__fig` slice rules in `facility-pin.css`.

### 4.3 IO-requeue (surviving var-clears and programmatic jumps)
A pin effect that clears its variables on teardown can erase a flow writer's values, and a single programmatic scroll jump can land one frame before the writer's first pass. Register a small IntersectionObserver (generous `rootMargin`, e.g. `300px 0px`) AFTER the pin effect's own IO that re-schedules a rAF write — worked reference in `lifetime-reel-01.jsx` and `media-split-01.jsx`.

### 4.4 Single-column re-composition without touching desktop DOM
`display: contents` on the desktop wrapper elements + flex `order` on the children re-composes a 2-column section into one editorial column with zero DOM moves, so every desktop pin selector keeps working. Gotchas: the flex column MUST set `align-items: stretch` (an inherited `start` shrinks items to content width — this collapsed the film strip to 93px), and any base `display: none` for a flow-only element must sit BEFORE the media block that re-enables it or the cascade order silently wins the wrong way. Worked reference: the ≤900 block in `facility-pin.css`.

### 4.5 Position-sorted reveal cascades
Whenever CSS re-orders layout (`order`, `display:contents`, grid placement), sort animation targets by on-screen position (top, then left, ~24px tie tolerance; re-sort on resize) instead of DOM order, so the cascade sweeps what the eye sees — an element rendered above another must never animate in after it. Worked reference: `image-accordion-01.jsx`.

### 4.6 Reversed horizontal scroller
`flex-direction: row-reverse` on the scroller seats DOM item #1 at the RIGHT at rest (a row-reverse scroller natively rests at its first item, `scrollLeft` goes negative leftward), with later items extending off the left bleed; a left-to-right swipe travels 1→N. DOM and screen-reader order stay 1→N. Reverse the cascade thresholds to match the visual sweep (leftmost item arrives first, item #1 lands last at p=1). Worked reference: `.mw-capalt__list` in `facility-pin.css`.

### 4.7 Flow-mode in-panel insets
Write the inset block's media query as the EXACT COMPLEMENT of the pin gate (`(orientation: portrait), (max-width: …), (max-height: …), (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`) so every non-pinned surface gets real air between the panel edge and the first/last content row. Worked reference: the flow-inset block in `home/lifetime.css`.

### 4.8 Sticky touch-`:hover`
A tap sets `:hover` persistently on touch. Media-gate ALL hover-only choreography (spotlights, gathers, grows) off re-flowed surfaces — including rules that look harmless; two ungated gather rules slid photos 184px off-lattice on tap. Replace with tap-to-select + an outside-`pointerdown` clear, and re-tune any state that grows an element (a desktop 1.24× lattice grow overflows a phone column; it's capped at 1.04 ≤1024). Worked references: `sectors.css` frieze-surface media list + `sector-diamonds-04.jsx`; `media-split-01.jsx` activeCap clear.

### 4.9 CSS deletion audit
Before deleting CSS rules, grep what they were overriding/neutralizing — old fallback blocks are often kept inert ONLY by later neutralizing rules, and deleting the neutralizer re-activates the legacy block (exactly how a broken phone layout shipped). Prefer deleting the legacy block itself once grep proves it has no other consumer.

### 4.10 Derived geometry — multi-element compositions are computed, never eyeballed
Any composition of 3+ positioned elements (mosaic tiles, staggered label rows, gathered spotlight rows) gets its coordinates DERIVED on paper against explicit gutter targets (e.g. "every gap ≥3% of the stage, eyebrow clearance ≥16px") before a single CSS value is written, and then verified with an **exhaustive pairwise probe at the exact rest frame** (for every element pair, assert separation ≥ N px on at least one axis; plus clearance assertions against neighbors like eyebrows/headers). Eyeballed offsets shipped overlapping careers tiles twice — "looks roughly like a grid" in one screenshot is not a layout. Worked reference: the compact mosaic in `home/careers.css` + the overlap probe in `_scratch_resp_home/round5-verify.mjs`.

### 4.11 CSS custom-property scoping traps
- **Per-band custom props do not inherit sideways.** `--g` (the gutter/bleed var) is declared on specific band selectors; a NEW sibling band consuming `var(--g)` silently resolves it to nothing and the negative bleed margins become 0 — re-declare the formula on every new band that uses it (bit the mission-flow panel: "full-bleed" rendered guttered with zero errors anywhere).
- **Registered properties pin var() fallbacks.** A `@property` with `initial-value: 0` makes `var(--x, 1)` ALWAYS resolve (initial 0 wins; the fallback is dead) — a flow scrub consuming it rests INVISIBLE on no-JS/RM. Use a fresh UNREGISTERED var for flow writers (e.g. `--mflow-rev` instead of the registered `--mission-rev`).
- **Cascade order on display toggles (REPEAT OFFENDER — it was already in §4.4 and got re-made the same week).** A base `display:none` for a narrow-only element placed AFTER the media block that re-enables it silently wins. Before adding any base+override display pair, check source order; after adding it, probe `display` computed at both a wide and a narrow viewport.

### 4.12 Clamp/size audit — sizes must track the container they live in
Every `clamp()` and every fixed px size gets checked at the EXTREMES of the band it serves, against the narrowest CONTAINER it must fit — not just at the design reference width. The two killers: **fixed-px type inside a shrinking column** (a 16px caption that never scales), and **viewport-based clamps inside stepped columns** (7.2vw numerals while the column width jumps with a grid clamp — vw can't track both sides of a breakpoint step; use container-relative `cqi` units with `container-type: inline-size` on the band). And when content sits inside a clipped shape (chevron bands), probe **glyph-vs-clip-boundary clearance** — compute the clip edge at the glyph's own depth and assert the rightmost glyph clears it; the history stats' unit + caption were silently clipped at EVERY pinned width including 1440 and nobody had ever probed it. Where JS already knows the clip geometry, have it write the real clearance as a var (`--stats-safe-right` in `timeline-stats.jsx` setRedge) instead of guessing a padding clamp. Worked reference: the ≥1201-landscape stats block in `home/history.css` + the clearance probe in `_scratch_resp_home/stats-fit-verify.mjs`.

### 4.13 Entrance reveals on horizontal scrollers — one band, one entrance
A per-item reveal observer fires entrances MID-SWIPE on a horizontal strip (the off-screen items intersect only when scrolled to — the certs cards played their entrance while being swiped). On any h-scroll surface, the first item to cross the threshold fires the WHOLE band's entrance; horizontal position never gates a reveal. Worked reference: `tall-static-banner-01.jsx`.

### 4.14 Seamless −50% marquee loops
A doubled-list marquee wraps invisibly ONLY if the −50% translate equals exactly one set's width. Two killers: the track must be `width: max-content` (a default-width track makes the keyframe translate half the CONTAINER — a guaranteed snap every cycle), and item spacing must live on per-item trailing margins, never flex `gap` (N doubled items hold N−1 between-gaps, putting the wrap half a gap off). Probe it: `item[n/2].left − item[0].left === trackWidth/2` exactly. Speed: ~100–120px/s reads lively; the brand default 35s cycle reads static. Worked reference: the `.tl-marquee__track` overrides in `home/affiliates.css`.

### 4.15 Scrubbed transforms on SVG: composite the ELEMENT, not a group
A CSS transform on a `<g>` INSIDE an svg re-rasterizes everything in it every frame — a scrubbed parallax on a 280-tile group was visibly choppy. Put scrubbed/animated transforms on the `<svg>` element itself (or an HTML wrapper): replaced elements GPU-composite like any HTML box (`translate3d` + `will-change: transform`, oversize the layer so travel never exposes an edge). Split independently-moving groups into separate overlaid `<svg>` layers; `<use href="#id">` resolves document-wide, so defs can live in the first layer only. Worked reference: `.mw-final__wash-layer` in `home/final-cta.css` + `multi-column-cta-01.jsx`.

### 4.16 Tiled repeats of a complex SVG: defs + one-node uses
Never stamp a multi-hundred-line SVG component N times — define it ONCE in `<defs>` (`<g id>` wrapping the component) and instance it with one-node `<use transform="translate(…) scale(…)">` stamps (flip a row with a negative x-scale seated at `x + w·s`). 280 instances cost 280 DOM nodes, not 280 copies of the art. Var-tokened fills resolve from the consuming context — redeclare the token recipe on the wash container. Worked reference: `FinalTruckWash` in `multi-column-cta-01.jsx`.

## 5. Phase four — verify

Follow the root `CLAUDE.md` protocol ("How to verify responsive work") exactly. The measured-assertion menu that paid for itself this session: element-center offsets ("caption 0px off band center"), fits-inside checks ("active cluster bounds 17→373 inside 390"), computed-style probes for stray legacy rules, **exhaustive pairwise overlap/gutter probes for compositions (§4.10)**, and **exact-frame timing checks** (settle-loop the scroll until anchor element X sits at viewport position Y, then assert `p === 1.000` and the last item's opacity). Exercise interaction states per viewport (tap, then measure + screenshot). Capture mid-scrub frames and judge the motion by eye, not just the variables. Re-verify desktop byte-invariance every round. After ANY edit that touches block comments or JSX structure, immediately probe the page for a 500/compile overlay before measuring anything (a comment closed one paragraph early once shipped a dead page that read as "selector not found").

**Judge the key poses against the bar before declaring done.** For each viewport, render the section's REST pose, a MID-motion frame, and the SETTLED/LANDED pose, and explicitly ask of each: "is this an exceptional, high-end, expensive-looking website?" Condensed spacing, copy crowding its CTAs, compositions that read as accidental — these are bar failures even when every geometric assertion passes (the careers landed frame passed all probes and still failed the bar on spacing).

**A failing measured assertion is REAL until proven otherwise — at a proven frame.** The cardinal sin: the pairwise probe reported three desktop overlaps and they were dismissed as "mid-dive sampling artifacts" — two were a genuine regression that shipped to the reference viewport. The rule: never explain away a red probe with a plausible story; re-run it at a PROVEN exact frame (assert the frame itself first: `trackTop === 0`, `s0 === rest value`) and only a green re-run closes it. Corollary — **tweaks have cross-surface blast radius**: a desktop-side change made for a narrow-view reason (the aerial nudge for the centred eyebrow) re-opens the FULL composition probe on the original surface too, not just the surface you were fixing.

**Playwright probe hygiene** (every one of these produced a false reading this session):
- **One fresh browser per agent per run, always** (`chromium.launch()` — own instance, never a shared/reused page): another agent scrolling the same page corrupts both agents' measurements. Close it when done.
- **Approach pinned/auto sections forward-only** when testing their natural behavior — settle-loops that overshoot and correct UPWARD register as user scroll-up and cancel auto behaviors (`cancelled` latches until un-pin).
- **Programmatic teleports leave stale writer values** (a giant `scrollTo` then settle can show a section's vars frozen at the previous pose despite IO-requeue). Before declaring a writer broken, re-test with stepped real scrolls (~200–600px increments with waits) — that's also what a finger/wheel actually generates.
- **Geometry probes only at exact frames.** A composition probe taken mid-dive measures grown tiles and reports phantom overlaps; settle to the exact rest/anchor frame (and assert the frame: `trackTop === 0`, `s0 === rest`) before reading rects.
- Touch emulation: `{ viewport, isMobile: true, hasTouch: true }`, viewport screenshots only (element screenshots of taller-than-viewport nodes silently reset the emulation), and settle-loop scroll positioning because JS-sized sections above shift layout for a few frames.

**Parallelize the capture matrix.** The viewport matrix × poses is embarrassingly parallel: spawn one capture subagent per viewport (sonnet-class is plenty), each launching its OWN browser, walking its viewport's poses (including motion micro-frames via stepped scrolling), saving screenshots with a strict naming convention and returning a manifest + raw measurements. Subagents are CAMERA OPERATORS ONLY — they capture and measure, they do not judge; the main agent Reads every screenshot and makes every quality call itself. The `designing-responsive-sections` personal skill carries the full protocol.

## 6. Pre-ship checklist (the failure museum — every line is a shipped failure)

Run down this list before calling a section done; each item names the failure it prevents.

1. Desktop intent inventory written, including motion beats AND mounted props? (VBEC photo-wall; careers autoScroll)
2. Pin-vs-flow decided by the motion test, not a blanket rule? (careers un-pinned twice)
3. Logan's exact geometry words implemented literally? ("centered" ≠ "fully on screen")
4. His specific idea treated as input, full composition pass still done? (history round 1)
5. Multi-element geometry derived + pairwise-probed at the exact rest frame? (overlapping mosaic)
6. Motion contracts: land AT anchors, assembly visible the whole approach, perceptible by eye? (rail finishing early; invisible map zoom)
7. New full-bleed bands re-declare `--g`; flow vars unregistered; display-toggle cascade order checked? (§4.11 trio)
8. CSS deletions audited for what they neutralized? (legacy seal block)
9. Interaction states exercised per viewport with a way OFF sticky hover? (lattice overflow; spotlight fly-off)
10. Key poses judged against the "exceptional/expensive" bar — especially spacing? (condensed careers landing)
11. Seam rhythm consistent with the page (~110–130px perceived between sections)? (33px history→careers seam)
12. Every red probe re-run green at a proven frame — none explained away as artifacts? (dismissed desktop-overlap probe shipped a real regression)
13. Any tweak near the original surface re-probed on THAT surface too, not just the one being fixed? (desktop aerial nudge for the eyebrow broke the desktop grid)
14. Every clamp/fixed size checked at its band's extremes against its CONTAINER, and clipped-shape content probed glyph-vs-clip? (§4.12 — history stats overflowed by 1240 and were chevron-clipped at 1440 itself)
15. Geometry scrubs inside pinned sections complete AT the pin frame? (§3 — who-we-serve diamonds froze crooked through the hold)
16. Horizontal scrollers: entrances fire as one band, never per-item on h-intersection? (§4.13 — certs revealed mid-swipe)
17. Looping marquees wrap pixel-exact (`item[n/2].left − item[0].left === trackWidth/2`)? (§4.14 — the −50% snap)

## 7. Page-scale production — the next 50+ pages are NOT built like the home page

The home page was the EXPLORATION: bespoke choreography invented section by section, with weeks of back-and-forth paying for every pattern in this document. That cost is paid once. Every other page (50+ across miller-web and the TL49 conversion) spends the dividend — the `components-v2` templates are now responsive-hardened from the home pass, the recipes above are named and proven, and the motion budget for non-home pages is deliberately light (§0). **A page is a one-session job. If a page is turning into an expedition, something is being re-explored that shouldn't be.**

The per-page workflow:

1. **Compose, don't build.** miller-web interior pages are thin `page.jsx` files ordering existing `06_sections` templates fed by a `lib/content/<page>.js` module — the emergency-response page (DESIGN-SYSTEM §13) is the worked interior reference, `/template-gallery` is the catalog, `npm run template-map` is the blast-radius check. Reach for an existing template FIRST; extend via a default-preserving config knob second; a new `*-0N` template third; bespoke section markup never (home-only exception). TL49 pages mirror the Miller references per DESIGN-SYSTEM §14.
2. **Motion budget up front (§0):** pick the page's one or two scroll-tied moments from the existing vocabulary (a reveal, one scrub from §4) and write their §3 contracts in a sentence each. No new motion *mechanics* on interior pages — only new *applications* of proven ones. If a page seems to need a novel mechanism, stop and flag it (that's a home-tier design decision for logan).
3. **Templates are trusted; seams and content are not.** Hardened templates don't need per-page re-verification of their internals — what DOES need checking per page: section seam rhythm (~110–130px perceived), content-length edge cases (long titles/short copy in the template's clamps — §4.12 thinking), and the page's own motion moments.
4. **ONE verification pass, parallel, at the end** — not iterative rounds: spawn the per-viewport capture agents (skill protocol / §5) across the matrix (390 / 768 portrait / 1024 landscape / 1440 + any surface the page's templates gate on), run the §6 checklist against the artifacts, judge key poses against the bar, fix, re-verify only what changed. Budget: one capture round + one fix round.
5. **Imagery: GENERATE, don't hunt (logan 2026-06-11 — the standard for page design).** Instead of reusing the existing photo library, request ultra-realistic, high-end images purpose-fit to the design via the codex MCP (`image_gen`) or codex CLI (on Windows the MCP call MUST use `sandbox: danger-full-access` + `approval-policy: never` to save files). Need a transparent background? Request the background in the page's own bg color instead — logan cuts it out later if he wants. Any image featuring company trucks: attach reference photos from the `public/miller/miller-env-*` truck folders (freightliner-business-class-m2106-vaccum-B, 2018-volvo-vnl-760, 2020-international-lt625, ford-f250-super-duty-2022, peterbilt367-vaccum-C, kensworth-t800-vaccum-A) so the generated trucks match the real fleet. Generated images are placeholders logan can swap — design to the IDEAL image, not to what's lying around. **PHOTOS ARE PROMPTED NEUTRAL (logan's explicit instruction, original brief + restated 2026-06-12): subject + composition + format/aspect ratio + "high quality, shot by a professional photographer" — and NOTHING about color.** Do NOT impose a film grade, color scheme, time of day, "golden hour", sky color, or banned color families on photographic subjects; the no-blue palette lock (DESIGN-SYSTEM) governs UI tokens, chrome, and generated GRAPHICS — never photographs. A real site under a normal blue sky IS a real photo; a page of uniform amber golden-hour shots reads fake (this mistake shipped across three pages on 2026-06-12 before logan caught it — do not reintroduce it from older session notes). Hard-won riders from the CWC v2 pass: (a) **an object that must sit directly ON a section surface** (product/spec-sheet shots) should be requested on a PURE WHITE background and rendered with `mix-blend-mode: multiply` — white × surface = surface exactly, mathematically seamless, while "generate on the page's bg hex" always comes back a few RGB points off and leaves a visible rectangle; the blend needs a backdrop INSIDE the element's stacking context (a `z-index`ed inner wrapper isolates it — paint the surface color on the media box itself). (b) **Multi-image sets cohere via SETTING, not grade:** share one setting/realism block across the set's prompts (same locale flavor, same documentary register) and attach a finished set-sibling as a reference so late images match early ones — but never via imposed lighting/color directives. (c) Codex sometimes reports "done" without writing the file — verify with `Test-Path` + LastWriteTime after every call. (d) Codex output tops out at 1536px wide — full-bleed heroes will soften on retina; flag to logan rather than fake-upscaling.
6. **New pattern discovered while building a page?** It goes HERE (named recipe + worked reference) and into the template as a knob — never only into that page's CSS comments. Point-of-use comments are breadcrumbs; this document is the canon.

When you evolve one of these patterns intentionally, update this doc and `docs/DESIGN-SYSTEM.md` §9/§11 in the same change.

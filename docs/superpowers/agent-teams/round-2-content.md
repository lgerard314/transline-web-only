# Round 2 ‚Äî Content strategy review

## Verdict

**Conditional accept.** The IA and trust-signal work in v2 is genuinely good, but the spec still treats Miller as a services-grid-plus-trust-strip site. The actual operating story ‚Äî a 64-hectare licensed treatment facility, named after Vaughn Bullough, that processes both organic and inorganic waste at one location ‚Äî is the rarest asset Miller owns and the spec under-uses it. Three structural narrative decisions need to be made before phase 2 starts: (1) what the flagship page actually is, (2) whether VBEC is a place or a person, and (3) whether `ServiceDetailTemplate` can survive 11 pages of wildly mismatched copy depth.

## Strongest narrative concerns

1. **Flagship mismatch ‚Äî Remediation gets the bespoke template; Treatment carries the operating story.** ¬ß3.3 builds a `RemediationTemplate` (6-card "What We Do", 5-step `<ol>`, 4 case studies, "Why Choose Miller", callback form). ¬ß3.2 collapses Industrial Waste Treatment into the generic `ServiceDetailTemplate` with "one or two prose sections" + bullets. But `06-svc-industrial-waste-treatment.md` is the wall: six processing categories, ~30 capabilities, the only mention of SR&ED federal funding, the only place "treat industrial organic and inorganic waste both in liquid and solid forms at one location" appears. Remediation is the *newer sales page*; Treatment is the *operating moat*. **Decision the spec must make:** either promote Treatment to its own bespoke template (`TreatmentTemplate` with the 6 process categories as accordion or tabbed sections), OR widen `ServiceDetailTemplate` to a "capabilities matrix" variant. Do not let Treatment render as two prose paragraphs on a remediation-shaped page.

2. **VBEC is a person, not an acronym.** ¬ß3.1 step 6 puts "Vaughn Bullough Environmental Centre" in an eyebrow. ¬ß3.6 puts the VBEC name in the headline. Neither tells the story `13-about.md` actually tells: Vaughn Bullough joined as GM in 1997, gave the company "steady hand, tremendous vision", and the facility was *renamed for him* by Blair McArthur. That's the rarest brand asset on the site ‚Äî a facility named after a real, named human being. **Decision:** the About page (¬ß3.5) needs a dedicated "The Vaughn Bullough story" section preserving the renaming-announcement framing, AND the treatment-facility hero should say "named for Vaughn Bullough, who built it" ‚Äî not just expand the acronym. If the spec treats VBEC as a four-letter brand mark, the most defensible piece of Miller's history evaporates.

3. **HomeTemplate step ordering buries the operating story.** ¬ß3.1 sequence: hero ‚Üí trust strip ‚Üí services grid ‚Üí "Over 25 years" ‚Üí mission ‚Üí VBEC ‚Üí stats ‚Üí careers ‚Üí marquee ‚Üí final CTA. By the time a procurement visitor reaches VBEC (step 6) they've already scrolled past ISO certs, 10 service cards, and two editorial blocks. The procurement journey (¬ß2.1 Journey B) is "vendor evaluation, multi-visit" ‚Äî they want to see the *facility*. **Decision:** move "Our Facility (VBEC)" up to step 4 (immediately after the services grid), so the sequence reads positioning ‚Üí proof ‚Üí what we do ‚Üí where we do it ‚Üí why we exist (mission) ‚Üí stats ‚Üí careers. The mission block is editorial filler between services and facility; it belongs lower.

4. **`ServiceDetailTemplate` is a Procrustean bed for 10 mismatched pages.** Copy volume by scrape: Industrial Cleaning = 2 short paragraphs (05); Industrial Waste Treatment = 6 processing categories with 30+ bullets (06); Emergency Response, Project Management, Stewardship are short; R&D and Specialty Recycling are medium. One template forcing "PageHero + 1-2 prose sections + optional bullets + Related Services rail + Emergency CTA" will pad the thin pages with whitespace and amputate the rich pages. **Decision:** `ServiceDetailTemplate` should ship with two declared variants ‚Äî `compact` (Industrial Cleaning, Vacuum Truck, Stewardship: hero + lead + bullet list + related rail) and `capabilities` (Treatment, R&D, Specialty Recycling: hero + lead + grouped capability sections + related rail). Spec should name which service goes in which variant in ¬ß3.2 or in `lib/services.js`.

5. **Processes as a top-level nav can't be defended at the content layer.** ¬ß2.2 keeps Processes as a primary nav item pointing at a single child (Disposal of Inorganic Oxidizers). The ¬ß10 open question acknowledges this. Narratively, `12-process-inorganic-oxidizers.md` is an *operational detail of Industrial Waste Treatment* ‚Äî it describes what happens inside VBEC. Until a second process page exists, this nav slot is a permanent one-item dropdown that signals "we ran out of structure." **Decision:** fold Processes into Industrial Waste Treatment as a "Specialized Processes" section linking to the oxidizers page, and reclaim the nav slot. If R&D ever publishes a second process write-up, restore the top-level item. The spec should make the call now, not defer it.

6. **Careers `IndexTemplate` flattens the "join the family" voice.** ¬ß3.7 routes `/careers/` through the generic `IndexTemplate` (PageHero + card grid). But `22-careers.md` is two job titles, and `24-careers-working-at-miller.md` carries the actual employer-brand prose: "we don't just talk about sustainability; we live it" + four core values + DEI program + EAC. A bare card grid for two positions reads as "we are not hiring." **Decision:** `/careers/` needs a real narrative spine ‚Äî hero ‚Üí "Why Miller" 4-card (the four core values from 24) ‚Üí "Open Positions" (the 2 cards) ‚Üí benefits teaser ‚Üí DEI block ‚Üí "Send your resume to hr@" close. Either widen `IndexTemplate` to accept this content, or give Careers its own `CareersTemplate`.

7. **Quality Assurance is the strongest single sentence on the site and the spec treats it as a hero `lead`.** "The only hazardous waste management company in Canada with an integrated management system featuring three ISO certifications" (17). ¬ß3.1 step 2 surfaces it in the home eyebrow; ¬ß3.5 puts it in the QA page hero. Both correct. **But** it should also appear verbatim on every service page that touches regulated waste (Treatment, Remediation, Emergency Response, Stewardship, Specialty Recycling) as a small inline trust badge near the lead ‚Äî not just on home and QA. **Decision:** add to the deliberate-repetition list and route through `lib/content/brand.js` alongside "Over 25 years".

## Deliberate repetition list

Phrases that should appear on multiple pages by design, sourced from `lib/content/brand.js`:

- **"Over 25 years"** ‚Äî already centralised. Keep on: home hero/trust, Remediation lead (verbatim in scrape), About hero, every service page lead optionally.
- **"Safe, dependable, and on-time service"** ‚Äî appears in home hero + "For Over 25 Years" + scrape calls it a "messaging refrain". Should anchor: home hero subhead/supporting line, footer tagline, contact-page hero. Should NOT bleed onto service-detail pages (they have their own leads).
- **"The only hazardous waste management company in Canada with an integrated management system featuring three ISO certifications"** ‚Äî appears verbatim on: home trust eyebrow (¬ß3.1 step 2), QA hero (¬ß3.5), and as inline badge on Treatment + Remediation + Emergency Response + Stewardship + Specialty Recycling service pages.
- **"Cradle to grave"** ‚Äî say it on Home ("For Over 25 Years" block, verbatim) and on QA (in the tracking-database paragraph, verbatim). Do NOT repeat elsewhere ‚Äî it loses meaning.
- **24/7 emergency phone (204) 957-6327** ‚Äî repeat by design on every page where `EmergencyBanner` shows (¬ß2.4 allow-list), plus the contact page as the Phone card. The banner's job is repetition; the prose's job is not. Don't repeat it in service-page body copy.
- **"Vaughn Bullough Environmental Centre (VBEC)"** ‚Äî say the full name once per page on first use, then "VBEC" thereafter. Don't shorten in headlines on the treatment-facility page or About page ‚Äî those are the two pages that earn the full name.

**Say-once** phrases the spec should resist re-using: the full mission statement (home only), the full renaming-announcement framing (About only), the four core-values list (Working at Miller + Vision/Mission/Values only ‚Äî not on home).

## Voice & tone call

Miller should adopt a **dry, technical, slightly institutional voice** ‚Äî the voice of a 30-year operator who runs a licensed facility, not the voice of a sustainability brand. The scrape evidence: "We operate a fleet of vacuum trucks and pressure washer units to clean tanks and other industrial equipment" (05) ‚Äî flat, declarative, no adjectives. "Miller formulates chemistries for contaminated soils and hazardous solids" (06) ‚Äî verb-first, capability-led. Compare to TL49's more editorial cadence ("the 49th parallel runs through every shipment"); Miller does not need that. The brand asset is competence and licensure, not voice-of-customer warmth. **Recommendation:** keep Miller deliberately industrial ‚Äî short declarative sentences, capability lists over narrative paragraphs, named certifications over adjectives ("ISO 45001:2018" not "rigorously safe"). Reserve one editorial register for two places only: the VBEC renaming story on About, and the "Join the Miller Family" block on home/careers. Everywhere else, let the work speak.

# Case Study — Highway 16 Diesel Spill Response & Remediation — `/case-studies/highway-16-diesel-spill-response-remediation/`

## Hero
- **Headline:** Highway 16 Diesel Spill Response & Remediation
- **Lead:** On November 15, 2024, a highway tractor traveling on Highway 16 suffered a severe saddle tank rupture, spilling over 500 liters of diesel fuel into a roadside ditch. The risk of diesel leaching into nearby soil and water sources was high, prompting Manitoba Public Insurance (MPI) to dispatch an immediate cleanup effort.

## Background
A tow truck company initially created a makeshift sand berm to contain the spill, but specialized intervention was needed. Miller Environmental was engaged for emergency containment and a comprehensive remediation plan. Impacted area ≈ 45 m long × 9 m wide.

## The Challenge
- **Multiple Stakeholders:** Site near a CPKC railway line, a buried fiber cable (Bell MTS), and an active highway. Miller coordinated with provincial authorities, MPI, the railway, and utility companies.
- **Sensitive Excavation Conditions:** Soft-dig hydro vac trucks required to remove contaminated soil without damaging utility lines.
- **Regulatory Approval:** Comprehensive Remedial Action Plan (RAP) submitted to the province, meeting Canadian Council of Ministers of the Environment (CCME) guidelines.
- **Traffic & Site Management:** Temporary Highway 16 lane closures with specialized traffic control.

## Miller's Approach

### Emergency Containment & Water Removal
- **Pumping & Disposal.** 28,000 liters of diesel-contaminated water removed; more robust barrier system installed.

### Regulatory Compliance & Coordination
- **Remedial Action Plan.** Meets CCME standards for petroleum hydrocarbon impact. Soil sampling, excavation, and confirmatory lab testing.
- **Stakeholder Engagement.** Provincial regulators, MPI, CPKC Railway, Bell MTS.

### Soil Excavation & Testing
- **Hydro Vac Technology.** Soft excavation avoids underground utilities.
- **PID Meters & Lab Analysis.** Real-time hydrocarbon sniffers + lab confirmation.

### Site Restoration & Sign-Off
- **Backfill & Regrading.** Restored to original grade once confirmatory samples cleared.
- **Final Documentation.** Reports + test results submitted to the province; official sign-off in 60 days.

## The Results

| Benefit | Outcome |
| ------- | ------- |
| Rapid Emergency Response | Containment and large-volume pumping prevented further ditch / soil contamination |
| Regulatory Compliance | Government-approved RAP satisfied CCME standards for petroleum hydrocarbons |
| Safe & Sensitive Excavation | Hydro vac trucks avoided buried utility lines and fiber optic cables near the railway |
| Stakeholder Coordination | Collaboration with MPI, provincial authorities, CPKC Railway, Bell MTS |
| Clear Communication & Reporting | Comprehensive documentation; swift provincial sign-off within 60 days |
| Minimal Public Disruption | Effective temporary traffic management |

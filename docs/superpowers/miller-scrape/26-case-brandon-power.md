# Case Study — Brandon Power Facility Lime Dust Remediation — `/case-studies/brandon-power-facility/`

## Hero
- **Headline:** Brandon Power Facility Lime Dust Remediation
- **Lead:** A Silo Rupture and an Urgent Threat to Safety and Operations

## The Problem
In January 2023, Miller Environmental visited a natural gas-fired power plant in Brandon, Manitoba where a lime silo ruptured after an undetected leak, releasing lime powder that "spread extensively throughout the plant, contaminating four of the five floors." Affected areas included walls, structural supports, stairwells, mechanical hoists, and interior piping systems.

Key hazards:
- Dry lime becoming "highly exothermic" when exposed to water, creating chemical burn and fire risks (concerning given spring melt timing).
- "Inhalation of airborne lime dust posed a substantial risk to worker health, particularly the respiratory system."
- Large building sections temporarily unusable.

The facility needed "a swift and technically sound solution to ensure the facility could be remediated safely" while avoiding disruption to active plant operations.

## Choosing A Path Forward
Engaged Miller for "hazardous material containment," "site remediation," and "health and safety planning." Site-specific plan from March 2023.

## Miller's Approach

**Plan Development and Execution.** "Industrial-grade dry vacuum systems equipped with HEPA filters" extracted lime dust without generating airborne spread. Affected areas placed "under controlled negative pressure to prevent cross-contamination with operational zones." Material sealed in IBC containers for transport and disposal.

**Oversight and Collaborative Execution.** "An independent third-party hygienist" (Pinchin Ltd.) provided air quality testing, negative pressure verification, and site procedure inspection. Cleanup floor by floor in coordination with the client's engineers, operations teams, and health & safety staff.

**Outcomes and Project Closeout.** "Miller Environmental completed the remediation phase with no safety incidents and with full regulatory compliance."

## The Results

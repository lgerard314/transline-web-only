          Executive Summary   
          The evidence supports a clear conclusion:             White Owl is the post-Colas family-office and investment platform through which the McArthur and Carrick families continued to hold and manage major retained assets, especially Miller Waste Systems, Miller Environmental, Miller Transit, real-estate holdings, and related environmental businesses    . The strongest primary evidence comes from two official documents. First, the             Town of Grimsby’s 2019 news release         states that             White Owl Limited is “a private Canadian investment company controlled by the McArthur and Carrick families”         and identifies Miller Waste Systems and Miller Environmental Corporation as White Owl subsidiaries/affiliates in the waste sector. Second, a             White Owl Properties Limited submission to Ontario’s Environmental Registry         states that White Owl Properties is             “the real estate arm of the White Owl Family Office Group,”         that the family office was established to manage assets held by the             Carrick and McArthur families    , and that             since the 2018 sale to Colas    , the family office has allocated assets among private equity, public investments, and real property while retaining             outright ownership of Miller Waste Systems Inc. and Miller Transit Limited    .             [1]    
          The current operating platform is branded             White Owl Family Office Group         and presents itself as a private Canadian investment firm with offices in             Markham, Ontario         and             Tampa, Florida    , focused on             real estate, private businesses, and public/private equity and debt funds    . Its website says White Owl owns majority and minority positions across             building products, environmental and waste management, logistics, industrial manufacturing, financial technology services, and automotive         sectors, and that the group has acquired             more than 70 companies         over time.             [2]    
          What is             less clear         is the exact legal architecture beneath the brand. The accessible primary sources show at least three White Owl-named legal entities or legally-framed names in use:             White Owl Family Office Group Limited    ,             White Owl Properties Limited    , and             White Owl Limited    . A fourth label,             White Owl Corporate Group    , appears in trademark and address materials, but I could not verify from an official registry record during this research pass that it is itself a distinct legal entity rather than a mailing style or office descriptor. Publicly indexed official registry pages were not consistently retrievable for every Ontario corporation, so             registration numbers, incorporation dates, and statuses remain incomplete for some entities    .             [3]    
          On the operating side, the retained “Miller” cluster is well evidenced.             Miller Waste Systems         is described by White Owl’s 2022 ERO submission as             outright owned    ;             Miller Environmental Corporation         is identified by official Grimsby materials as owned within the same White Owl/Miller family structure;             Miller Transit Limited         is likewise described as outright owned and, in 2025–2026, had a Canadian trademark application filed by             White Owl Family Office Group Limited    ;             Escarpment Renewables         was acquired in 2019 by             White Owl Limited         through a subsidiary and later described as             Miller Waste’s         Grimsby green-energy facility before being sold with another biogas asset to Walker Industries in 2026.             [4]    
     
     
          Ownership, control, and inferred structure   
          The two most probative ownership documents are unusually direct. The             Town of Grimsby’s 2019 release         says White Owl Limited is             controlled by the McArthur and Carrick families    . The             White Owl Properties 2022 ERO submission         says the White Owl Family Office Group was established to manage assets held by the             Carrick and McArthur families    , names the large road/infrastructure businesses sold to Colas, and states that White Owl still held             outright ownership of Miller Waste Systems Inc. and Miller Transit Limited         after the 2018 Colas divestiture. Those two documents, taken together, strongly support the conclusion that White Owl is the retained family-wealth and control platform behind the Canadian Miller waste/environmental businesses.             [1]    
          The historical backdrop also fits. Colas announced in August 2017 that it had signed a share purchase agreement to acquire             100% of the equity interest in the Miller and McAsphalt group    , and Miller/McAsphalt’s own announcement identified             Blair McArthur    ,             John Carrick    , and             Kelly Carrick         as continuing leaders following that deal. White Owl’s later ERO filing describes the relevant infrastructure-company sale as having occurred in             2018    , after which the family office managed retained assets.             [5]    
          The newer IP record is also meaningful. In late 2025,             White Owl Family Office Group Limited         filed a Canadian trademark application for             MILLER TRANSIT & Design    , suggesting that the current parent platform is not merely a passive investment office but also the active legal applicant behind retained Miller operating brands. Miller Environmental’s own materials likewise say             TransLine 49° Environmental Services         is             owned by White Owl Family Office Group         and is an affiliate of Miller Environmental.             [6]

          Gaps, unverified claims, and assumptions   
          The biggest gap is             corporate-registry completeness    . I was able to verify White Owl activity through             official filings, regulatory notices, trademark records, and municipal/White Owl documents    , but I was             not able in this pass to retrieve a complete official registry record         for every Ontario White Owl entity. That means             incorporation dates, Ontario/federal jurisdiction, and registry IDs remain incomplete         for             White Owl Limited         and             White Owl Properties Limited    , and only partly visible for             White Owl Family Office Group Limited    .             [77]    
          A second unresolved issue is the             exact internal holding chain         from the White Owl parent platform to each Miller operating company. The 2022 ERO filing proves retained ownership of             Miller Waste Systems Inc.         and             Miller Transit Limited    , and the 2019 Grimsby release proves control of the platform by the McArthur and Carrick families. But the sources available here do not expose a formal share-register tree showing every intermediate holding company above             Miller Environmental Corporation    ,             Escarpment Renewables    , or             TransLine 49    .             [78]    
          The assumptions used in this report are narrow and explicit. I treat             “White Owl,” “White Owl Group,” and “White Owl Family Office Group”         as references to the same             umbrella family-office platform    , because the current official site uses those forms interchangeably in branding and page titles. I treat             White Owl Limited         as a             historical or parallel legal entity         within that platform, not necessarily the same corporation as             White Owl Family Office Group Limited    , because the current sources do not prove a rename or amalgamation. I treat             White Owl Corporate Group         as an             unverified mailing/office descriptor    , not a confirmed legal entity. I also exclude             WT Miller Holdings Ltd.         from the White Owl structure because, despite the similarity of name, I did not find reliable evidence connecting it to the McArthur/Carrick White Owl platform in the source set.             [79]    
          If you want the single most defensible bottom line, it is this:             White Owl is best understood as the McArthur/Carrick family-office platform that sat behind the retained Miller waste/environmental/transit and real-estate businesses after the Colas transaction, with White Owl Family Office Group Limited as the clearest current legal expression of that platform in the accessible federal filing record.             [80]

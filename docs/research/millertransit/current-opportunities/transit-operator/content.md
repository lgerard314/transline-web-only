## [H2] Responsibilities
[LI] Performs pre-trip and post-trip inspections of assigned vehicle; sets correct destination and route signs
[LI] Operates various types of vehicles, as assigned, safely, in all types of weather and traffic conditions, according to printed passenger schedules and Operators schedules
[LI] Arrives and signs in on time; works hours as scheduled; wears clean, neat uniform
[LI] Picks up and discharges passengers at designated bus stops safely; regulates heating and ventilating equipment on the bus for the comfort of passengers
[LI] Follows fare collection procedures which includes INIT, GFI, farebox, and Presto; guards against transfer abuse;
[LI] Reports all accidents, incidents, or unusual occurrences to dispatch office immediately; submits written reports on accidents, incidents or unusual occurrences as required
[LI] Accurately completes all reports
[LI] Follows rules and regulations of the Company
[LI] When the automatic stop calling is in operative, driver must call out the stop
[LI] Provides transit information to passengers as needed
## [H2] Requirements
[P] Education, Licensing, and Certifications Required:
[LI] Be legally entitled to work in Canada (eg. Canadian citizen, landed immigrant or valid work permit.)
[LI] Hold a valid BZ driver’s license in good standing
[LI] A clean driver’s abstract and commercial vehicle operator’s registration is required
[LI] Possess a Grade 12 diploma or equivalent
[P] Experience:
[LI] Previous bus or other heavy-duty vehicle driving experience with a minimum of six (6) months experience
[P] Skills and Knowledge Required:
[LI] Must possess strong verbal and written English language skills
[LI] Excellent customer service skills and communicating with the public
[LI] Willing to work split shifts, night shifts, weekend shifts and holidays on a rotating basis
[LI] You must own or have access to reliable transportation to come and go from work
[P] Physical Requirements:
[LI] Must pass Pre-Employment Drug & Alcohol Screening Test and Criminal Background Check
[LI] The individuals interested in this position must be capable of meeting the physical demands of the job. Those demands include but are not limited to: sitting for prolonged periods of time, using extreme motion such as gripping, reaching, push/pull of steering wheel, twisting and turning of neck and back, sensory/perceptual demands along with good concentration, visual acuity and colour vision deemed safe for driving to judge distances, fine finger movements such as keying in data in the farebox keypad.
[P] WHAT TO EXPECT in the role of Transit Driver with Miller Transit:
[LI] You will operate various conventional or articulated buses on routes and time schedules or charters as required.
[LI] You will be responsible for collecting fares, responding to customer inquiries concerning transit information and ensuring the safety of all customers.
[LI] You will be required to consistently demonstrate at all times attentiveness, good judgement, safe driving habits, and an ability to deal effectively and professionally with all customers.
[LI] As each day is unique, flexibility is required of this job; this is a unionized position
[P] Interested applicants are requested to forward a resume to Human Resources at: hr@millertransit.ca.
[P] We would like to thank all candidates who apply, however only those selected for an interview will be contacted.

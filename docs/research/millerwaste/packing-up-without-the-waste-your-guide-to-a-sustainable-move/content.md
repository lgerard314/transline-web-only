# [H1] Packing Up Without the Waste: Your Guide to a Sustainable Move
[P] By Miller Waste
[P] Moving to a new home or office is a fresh start, but it doesn’t have to come with a mountain of waste. Between cardboard boxes, packing materials, and unwanted items, relocations can create an overwhelming amount of garbage. The good news is that with a little planning and the right mindset, you can move sustainably while keeping waste to a minimum.
[P] At Miller Waste, we believe in waste-free solutions that help protect our planet. Before you start taping up another box, check out these creative ways to pack smarter, declutter responsibly, and recycle efficiently.
[IMG] photos/ff2b6e091b45__two-men-loading-a-piece-of-furniture-onto-a-moving-2024-10-1.jpg  (alt: "", natural 550×367)
[P] Two men loading a piece of furniture onto a moving truck.
## [H2] Choose Reusable Moving Bins Instead of Cardboard
[P] Cardboard boxes pile up fast. Instead of spending money on single-use boxes, opt for durable, reusable moving bins. These stackable bins keep your belongings secure, reduce waste and eliminate the need for endless rolls of packing tape. They are stronger than cardboard, reduce clutter after the move, and create zero waste since they can be reused.
[P] If using reusable bins isn’t an option, consider sourcing free used cardboard boxes from local stores or community groups. Once you’re done with them, be sure to recycle or pass them on to someone else who is moving.
## [H2] Pack Smarter with What You Already Have
[P] There’s no need to buy rolls of bubble wrap when you already have plenty of soft, protective materials at home. Use clothing, towels, blankets, and bedding to wrap fragile items like dishes and glassware. Socks and shoes can be stuffed inside glasses and vases for extra cushioning. Instead of packing books and small kitchen items in separate boxes, use suitcases and laundry baskets to transport them. These simple swaps reduce waste while keeping your belongings safe.
## [H2] Declutter with Purpose: Sell, Donate, or Recycle
[IMG] photos/d46cc571eb62__pexels-julia-m-cameron-6995179-2048x1365.jpg  (alt: "A donation bin full of clothes. ", natural 224×24)
[P] A donation bin full of clothes.
[P] Moving is the perfect opportunity to reassess what you truly need. Before throwing things in the trash, consider selling, donating, or recycling. Gently used furniture and appliances can be given to charities or sold online. Clothing and shoes can be donated to thrift stores or textile recycling programs. Books and office supplies may be accepted by schools and libraries. Electronics and old office equipment should be properly disposed of through an authorized e-waste recycling centre. Decluttering responsibly not only lightens your load but also gives your old items a second life. Read more on how to reduce clothing waste in our blog.
## [H2] Plan Your Waste and Recycling Strategy
[P] A move can generate a lot of waste, so setting up a waste station can help keep things organized. Designate separate bins for recyclables, reusable materials, landfill waste, and hazardous items like old paint, batteries, and cleaning chemicals. For a hassle-free way to dispose of your household waste, consider one of Miller Waste’s roll-off bins, delivered directly to your driveway.
[P] If you have large or bulky items that can’t be repurposed, bring them to a Miller Waste Drop-off Depot or your local waste management site, where they will properly dispose of it.
## [H2] Unpack with Sustainability in Mind
[P] Once you arrive at your new home or office, it’s important to dispose of moving materials responsibly. Break down and recycle cardboard boxes or offer them to someone else who may need them. Save reusable materials like bins and packing paper for future use. Avoid letting waste pile up by properly recycling plastics, e-waste, and any remaining packing materials. A clean, green move means less waste, less hassle, and a fresh start for both you and the environment.

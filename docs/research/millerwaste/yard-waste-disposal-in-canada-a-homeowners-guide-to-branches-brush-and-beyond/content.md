# [H1] A Homeowner’s Guide to Yard Waste: Branches and Brush
[P] By Miller Waste
[P] Cleaning up yard waste after a weekend of pruning or a surprise windstorm shouldn’t be a headache. Yet many Canadians are unsure where those piles of branches belong or when the city will collect them.
[P] Yard waste is picked up only on designated dates that align with your municipality’s collection schedule. Always check your local calendar or waste look-up tool before hauling bundles to the curb, because regulations can vary block by block. (e.g., Waterloo Region’s Waste Whiz, Toronto’s TOwaste, Hamilton’s Waste App, Guelph Waste, Ottawa Collection Calendar).
[P] In this guide, we break down disposal options that keep your yard tidy and protect the planet.

[P] Homeowner cleaning up yard waste.
## [H2] What Counts as Yard Waste?
[P] Yard waste can often be found in your backyard and includes:
[LI] Branches, twigs, brush and hedge trimmings
[LI] Small trunks and logs (diameter less than 10 cm)
[LI] Stumps and root balls under local size limits
[LI] Natural Christmas trees (undecorated)
## [H2] Not Yard Waste:
[LI] Treated lumber
[LI] Painted wood
[LI] Soil
[LI] Sod
[LI] Plastic pots
[LI] Rocks
[LI] Or pet waste
[P] Miller Tip: If it didn’t grow in your yard, it doesn’t belong in the yard waste pile.
## [H2] Preparation Checklist for Your Yard Waste
[LI] Cut it to size: Trim branches to less than 1.2 m (4 ft) long and less than 7 cm (3 in) thick.
[LI] Bundle or bag: Tie branches with natural twine; use paper yard bags for leaves and twigs.
[LI] Keep it dry: Store bags under a cover to prevent tears and keep them from getting waterlogged and exceeding the 20 kg (44 lb) limit.
[LI] Label containers: Mark reusable bins “Yard Waste” on both sides.

## [H2] Common Limitations When It Comes to Yard Waste
[LI] Weight cap of approximately 20 kg (44 lb) per bag or bundle.
[LI] Oversized stumps or treated wood are not accepted curbside
[LI] Contractors must haul away commercial quantities
[LI] Many cities pause yard‑waste service from mid‑December to early spring.
## [H2] Best-Practice Curbside Tips
[LI] Use paper yard bags or clearly labelled bins, no plastic bags.
[LI] Bundle branches less than 1.2 m (4 ft) long and 7 cm (3 in) thick, with a total weight of 20 kg (44 lb). Check local limits before you cut.
[LI] Remove contaminants like soil, rocks, or plastic.
[LI] Set out by 7 a.m. on collection day.
## [H2] Composting: Turn Branches Into Garden Gold
[P] Miller Waste Sytems operates Miller Compost facilities in Richmond Hill, Pickering, and Clarington that collectively process well over 100 000  tonnes of leaves and tree debris each year.
### [H3] How It Works
[LI] Receive and inspect: We remove potential contaminants.
[LI] Grind and windrow: Branches are chipped, piled, and aerated.
[LI] Hot composting: Compost reaches 55 °C+, killing weed seeds and pathogens.
[LI] Screen and cure: The finished material is dark, earthy, and crumbly.
[LI] Return to community: Material is sold to landscapers or given away on compost days.
[P] Resident Drop-Off: Richmond Hill accepts branches up to 30 cm in diameter and 1.8 m in length.
[P] Small twigs can be composted at home, but larger volumes are best handled by Miller Compost.

## [H2] Environmental Wins of Proper Yard Waste Disposal
[P] Proper yard-waste disposal diverts organic material from landfills and keeps nutrients in the soil.
[LI] Miller Compost processes over 100,000 tonnes of yard waste annually, transforming it into nutrient-rich compost for local use.
[LI] Compost returns nutrients to Canadian soils, supporting plant health, moisture retention, and reduced dependence on chemical fertilizers.
[LI] In 2022, Canada diverted 3.1 million tonnes of organics from landfill—yard waste made up a meaningful portion of that total.
[LI] Landfilled organics, including yard waste, still contributed to 23 Mt CO₂-e (3.3% of national GHG emissions) that year. Keeping organics out of landfills helps cut methane, a potent greenhouse gas.
[LI] Municipal programs that encourage yard-waste diversion also align with forthcoming organics-ban policies in several provinces.

## [H2] Frequently Asked Questions
### [H3] Question: Can I Toss Branches in My Garbage Bin?
[P] Answer: Generally, no. Nova Scotia and Prince Edward Island have province-wide bans, while many municipalities restrict yard waste in landfill-bound garbage. Use designated yard-waste programs.
### [H3] Question: What if the City Left My Bundles Behind?
[P] Answer: Check for tags that explain weight, size, or contamination issues, and correct them before the next pickup.
### [H3] Question: Do Contractors Get Curbside Service?
[P] Answer: Usually not. Commercial yard waste must be hauled privately unless your municipality allows contractor curbside pickup.

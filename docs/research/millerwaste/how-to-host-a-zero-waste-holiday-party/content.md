# [H1] How to Host a Zero-Waste Holiday Party
[P] By Miller Waste
[P] Holiday parties should be full of joy, not bags of garbage! Whether you’re celebrating at home or gathering at a restaurant, banquet hall, or other venue, there are plenty of ways to make your event more sustainable. Here are some easy, fun ways to celebrate while keeping things kind to the planet (with a bit of help from Miller Waste).
## [H2] 1. Send Digital Invitations
[P] Ditch the paper pile! Send your invites by email, text, or fun online tools like Paperless Post, Canva, or Evite. Add a warm note letting guests know you’re hosting a zero-waste celebration so they can feel part of it too.
[IMG] photos/24da02c4a6c5__image-1-2048x560.webp  (alt: "Holiday party, gingerbread-house design on pale green circle.", natural 452×24)
[P] Evite digital invitations.
## [H2] 2. Keep the Menu Simple and Low-Waste
[LI] Choose local, seasonal food with less packaging
[LI] Offer bulk snacks instead of single-serve packs
[LI] Cook just enough, and send leftovers home in reusable containers
[P] Tip: Take a relaxed stroll through your local farmer’s market. You’ll not only find fresh food, but you’ll also meet friendly faces and support your community. It’s a win-win!
[P] Bonus: Miller supports communities through organic waste collection that keeps food out of landfills and turns it into compost.
## [H2] 3. Choose Reusables Instead of Disposables
[P] Plastic plates? No thanks. Authentic dishes and cloth napkins instantly make your gathering feel special. Short on supplies? Borrow from a friend or rent a set. It’s easier (and often cheaper) than you think.
[P] Tip: Using a dishwasher is often more eco-friendly than using disposables, especially if it’s full. Just skip the pre-rinse.
[P] If you need to use disposables, choose compostable options made from bamboo, palm leaf, or sugarcane.
## [H2] 4. Decorate with Purpose
[LI] Reuse decorations you already have or can keep for future events
[LI] Try natural or DIY decor like pinecones, string lights, or dried fruit
[LI] Skip the single-use glitter and balloons, choose items you’ll actually want to keep and enjoy year after year.
[P] Choose items you actually want to keep and reuse again.
[IMG] photos/d7d72ddaeb0a__image2.webp  (alt: "Invitation category: Adult birthdays, floral invitation on peach-coloured circle.", natural 560×24)
[P] Eco-friendly decorating made simple.
## [H2] 5. Gift with Intention
[LI] Host a Secret Santa to reduce excess gifts (try Elfster or DrawNames)
[LI] Give homemade, local, or experience-based gifts
[LI] Wrap presents using scarves, fabric bags, or recycled paper
## [H2] 6. Set Up Clearly Labelled Waste Stations
[P] Help guests help you. Clearly label your bins for recycling, organics, and garbage so sorting is a no-brainer.  
 
Grab some tape and label your bins clearly:
[LI] Recycling
[LI] Organics (food scraps)
[LI] Garbage
[P] This works great at parties and is helpful for everyday use too. You can find these signs online as downloadable images.
[IMG] photos/e7d98df47b3e__image.webp  (alt: "A two-row grid of recycling signs. Top row shows large square signs: blue "PAPER" with a white recycling symbol, red "PLASTIC" with a white recycling symbol, yellow "CANS" with a white recycling symbol, and green "GENERAL" with a white waste-bin icon. Bottom row repeats smaller rectangular versions of the same four categories.", natural 1110×72)
## [H2] 7. Donate and Reuse After the Party
[P] Don’t toss good food. Pack leftovers for guests or donate to a local shelter or community fridge. Save your decorations to use again next year.
[P] A Greener Celebration Starts with Simple Changes
[P] Zero-waste doesn’t mean zero fun. With a few easy swaps and a little planning, you can host a holiday event that brings people together while protecting the environment.
[P] At Miller Waste Systems, we support homes, businesses, and communities that want to reduce waste and build a better future. From compost collection to recycling programs, we’re here to help.

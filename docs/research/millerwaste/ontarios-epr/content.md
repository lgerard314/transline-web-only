# [H1] Ontario’s EPR Is Reshaping Recycling—What It Means for Your Business
[P] By Miller Waste
## [H2] First, a quick clarification: “Producer” ≠ “every business”
[P] Ontario’s Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) shift makes product/packaging producers—brand owners, importers, and certain retailers/marketplaces—financially and operationally responsible for residential Blue Box recycling. See RPRA’s Blue Box producer guidance: RPRA – Producers
[P] This does not replace the waste and recycling services your business locations (IC&I: industrial, commercial & institutional) arrange with private haulers like Miller. Learn more about the residential scope here: RPRA – Blue Box Overview
[P] Bottom line: EPR changes who funds and runs the residential system by Jan 1, 2026, but your business pickup remains a private service you contract—Miller can set that up and optimise it.
## [H2] What’s changing in Ontario
[LI] Transition: Communities have been moving to a producer-run residential system July 1, 2023 → Dec 31, 2025. Full EPR applies January 1, 2026. Ontario – Producer Responsibility
[LI] Scope: EPR covers eligible residential sources. It does not cover IC&I waste streams at your place of business. RPRA – Blue Box Overview
[LI] Recent amendments: Ontario finalized changes for 2026+ that phase in or defer some recovery targets (e.g., flexible plastics). RPRA – Blue Box updates
## [H2] Why businesses should care—even if you’re not a “producer”
[LI] Continuity & cost control: Stable commercial pickups with a proven operator (Miller) help avoid service gaps and contamination surcharges.
[LI] Multi-site consistency: Standardise IC&I programs across locations (cardboard, organics, film, metal, glass, etc.).
[LI] If you are also a producer: You’ll still need RPRA registration/reporting and a PRO for the residential system—separate from your site’s IC&I service contract. RPRA – Register/Report
## [H2] Durham Region focus: what to expect locally

## [H2] A practical checklist for facility managers (IC&I)
[LI] Audit your streams (cardboard, organics, film plastics, metals, glass, e-waste, hazardous where applicable).
[LI] Right-size containers & pickup frequency to cut contamination and overflow fees.
[LI] Standardise multi-site programs (same signage, acceptable materials, training).
[LI] Capture cardboard and organics—usually the biggest, fastest ROI.
[LI] Add a film/plastics solution where volumes justify (baling or separate capture).
[LI] Measure & report: simple monthly diversion and contamination KPIs.
[LI] If you’re also a producer: complete RPRA registration/reporting and engage a PRO—separate track from your IC&I contract.
## [H2] How Miller Waste helps businesses—today
[LI] Commercial pickups: tailored garbage, recycling, organics programs for IC&I sites across Ontario.
[LI] Program design & training: bin standards, signage, staff onboarding to reduce contamination.

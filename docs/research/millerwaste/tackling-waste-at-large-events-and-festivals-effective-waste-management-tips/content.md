# [H1] Tackling Waste at Large Events and Festivals: Effective Waste Management Tips
[P] By Miller Waste
[IMG] photos/2a3c315aab1a__cleaning-volunteer-and-people-on-grass-with-trash-2025-04-06.jpg  (alt: "Waste at Large Events and Festivals", natural 264×24)
[P] Miller Waste Solutions For Events
[P] With summer just around the corner, the festival and outdoor event season is quickly approaching. From community festivals to large concerts, these gatherings bring people together to celebrate, take in live music, and enjoy delicious food. However, they also bring a significant challenge—waste. The amount of trash generated during these events can be overwhelming, with food containers, plastic bottles, and other single-use items piling up fast.
[P] As event organizers prepare for this busy season, it’s crucial to have a waste management plan in place. A thoughtful approach to waste reduction not only minimizes environmental impact but also enhances the overall event experience for attendees. In this blog, we’ll provide practical tips for reducing waste at large events and festivals, while showcasing how Miller Waste Systems’ waste management solutions can make your event greener, more sustainable, and hassle-free.
## [H2] Why Waste Management at Events Matters
[P] Large events offer important opportunities to promote environmental responsibility. Festivals can generate thousands of pounds of waste in just a few days—and without proper waste management, a significant portion ends up in wetlands or other vital habitats. In fact, only about 65% of waste produced at concerts is correctly disposed of. By prioritising proper waste diversion, event organisers can help reinforce the principles of recycling and sustainability for all attendees.
## [H2] Tips for Reducing Waste at Events
### [H3] 1. Implement a Comprehensive Recycling Program
[P] Providing separate recycling bins for attendees is a simple but effective way to encourage waste sorting. Ensure that bins are marked clearly to avoid contamination of recyclable materials.
### [H3] 2. Opt for Sustainable Food Packaging
[P] Single-use plastic containers are a major contributor to waste at events. By working with food vendors to provide compostable or reusable packaging, you can significantly reduce the amount of plastic waste generated.
### [H3] 3. Promote Digital Tickets and Information
[P] By eliminating paper tickets, flyers, and pamphlets, you not only reduce paper waste but also make the event eco-friendlier. Encourage attendees to use mobile tickets and share event details digitally.
### [H3] 4. Offer Waste Sorting Stations
[P] Set up clearly labeled waste sorting stations at key points throughout the event. Offer separate bins for organics, recyclables, and trash, and make sure staff or volunteers are available to help attendees properly dispose of their waste.

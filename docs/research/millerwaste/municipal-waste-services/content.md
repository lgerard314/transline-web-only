## [H2] Material Recovery and Recycling

[SECTION: section .elementor-section.elementor-top-section]
[IMG] photos/6e0610188170__NJB_2358.jpg  (alt: "Employees sort incoming recyclables at a Miller Waste Material Recovery Facility.", natural 360×240)
[IMG] photos/1058d1715a9d__Screen-Shot-2023-06-06-at-10.44.03-AM.png  (alt: "", natural 360×239)
[IMG] photos/6b806d56c182__MRF-Bales-14-4-2048x1367.jpg  (alt: "Paper is baled at a Miller Waste MRF.", natural 360×240)

[SECTION: section .elementor-section.elementor-top-section]
[P] Miller receives, sorts, and processes hundreds of thousands of recyclables annually from residential, commercial, and industrial sources. Our Material Recovery Facilities (MRFs) are outfitted with machinery that removes contaminants from incoming recyclable items and materials, including glass, aluminum, paper, and plastics.
[P] After being cleaned and separated, the material is baled and shipped to secondary processors and manufacturers who use the material to create new products, helping them reduce their reliance on virgin materials.

[SECTION: section .elementor-section.elementor-top-section]
## [H2] Benefits of Recycling

[SECTION: section .elementor-section.elementor-top-section]
### [H3] Extends the useful life of resources
[P] We lose the value of resources forever when they decompose in a landfill. Repurposing and refurbishing resources reduces our need to harvest raw material from the earth using harmful, unsustainable practices.
### [H3] Grows the economy and sparks creativity
[P] Reduced reliance on virgin material lowers businesses’ exposure to volatile raw material prices; demand for new products and services related to logistics, remarketing, refurbishment, and remanufacturing introduces new revenue streams.
### [H3] Improves health of people and planet
[P] By diverting material from the landfill, we prevent excess carbon dioxide and greenhouse gas emissions from negatively impacting water, air and soil quality.

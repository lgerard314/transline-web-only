# [H1] How to Manage Waste During Yard Work and Home Renovations
[P] By Miller Waste
[P] Whether you’re tackling a major home renovation project or sprucing up your yard, managing the waste generated can be a daunting task. With the volume of debris adding up quickly, it’s important to have a solid plan in place to dispose of your materials responsibly. Here’s how you can simplify waste management during yard work and renovations with the help of Miller Waste Systems, ensuring a sustainable approach to disposal and recycling.
[IMG] photos/fe0072ba5599__NJB_0120-2-scaled.webp  (alt: "roll off bin", natural 89×24)
[P] Miller Waste Systems 20-yard bin placed on a residential driveway in front of a house
## [H2] Why Proper Waste Management Matters for Yard Work and Renovations
[P] Did you know that Canadians dispose of around 729 kg of waste per person per year, with waste production spiking significantly during renovations? According to Statistics Canada, renovation projects contribute heavily to this number. The construction, renovation, and demolition (CRD) sector alone generate an astounding 4 million tonnes of waste annually in Canada. Even a seemingly small home project, like a kitchen remodel, can easily produce 1–2 tonnes of material waste.
[P] Given these statistics, it’s clear that homeowners should anticipate large volumes of waste and plan accordingly. One way to do this is by utilizing professional bin rental services to manage the debris and ensure proper disposal.
## [H2] Common Types of Waste You’ll Encounter
[P] Yard work and renovations often involve different types of waste, each requiring specific disposal methods. Miller Waste Systems makes it easy to dispose of:
### [H3] Branches and Yard Waste
[P] By using an organic diversion program, yard waste like branches, grass clippings, and leaves can be sent to composting facilities, keeping these materials out of the landfill.
### [H3] Soil and Gravel
[P] If you’re doing landscaping or excavating work, it’s important to ensure that soil and gravel are properly disposed of. Miller Waste offers services to handle these types of waste responsibly.
### [H3] Wood, Drywall, and Other Renovation Debris
[P] Wood, drywall, and other construction debris can often be recycled. By choosing Miller Waste for your renovation project, you can help ensure that materials are sorted for recycling, reducing the environmental impact of your project.
## [H2] Simplify Yard Work and Renovations with Miller Waste Bin Rentals
[P] One of the easiest ways to handle waste during yard work or home renovations is by renting a dumpster bin from Miller Waste Systems. Whether you’re clearing out branches, soil, wood, or demolition debris, a bin rental makes it easy to manage waste without the hassle of running to the dump multiple times.
[P] We offer a variety of bin sizes to accommodate your needs, from small yard cleanups to larger renovation projects. With convenient drop-off and pick-up services, you can focus on your project while we take care of the waste management.
## [H2] Choosing the Right Bin Size for Your Project
[P] Choosing the right bin size is crucial to ensure you don’t overpay for unused space or run out of room mid-project. Here are our recommended bin sizes for common yard work and renovation tasks:
### [H3] 14-yard bin
[P] This is the ideal choice for smaller home renovation projects or light yard work, such as clearing away branches, soil, and small landscaping debris. It’s perfect for bathroom remodels, small kitchen renovations, or a garage cleanout.
### [H3] 20-yard bin
[P] This bin size is great for larger home renovation projects, including full kitchen or bathroom remodels, or for larger landscaping projects like tree removal or heavier debris such as drywall and wood. It’s also useful for medium-sized demolition projects and larger home cleanouts.
### [H3] 40-yard bin
[P] For substantial renovations or full home demolition, this is the go-to bin size. It’s also suitable for significant landscaping projects or when you’re clearing out multiple rooms or large quantities of construction materials, such as wood, drywall, and roofing debris.
[P] When in doubt, our team can help you determine the right size for your specific project needs.

## [H2] Best Practices for Recycling and Organics Diversion
[P] If you choose not to rent a bin from Miller Waste Systems, one of the easiest ways to reduce your environmental impact during yard work or home renovations is by sorting your waste for recycling and organics diversion. It’s essential to separate materials, as this helps ensure more waste is recycled or composted rather than being sent to the landfill.
### [H3] Here’s how to get started with sorting your materials:
[LI] Wood and Metals: Scrap wood, metal pipes, and wires can often be recycled, which reduces the need for new resources.
[LI] Cardboard, Plastics, and Glass: These common renovation waste items can be sent to recycling facilities, where they are reused in the manufacturing of new products.
[LI] Organic Materials: Yard waste like leaves, grass, and small branches can be composted, creating nutrient-rich soil for future use in gardens and landscaping.
[P] Keep in mind, sorting waste properly can take time and effort. Without the convenience of our bin rentals, the process of ensuring proper diversion from the landfill can be more cumbersome.

[P] Home renovation and yard work projects can be exciting, but they also come with the responsibility of managing waste properly. With the significant volume of waste produced during these projects, choosing the right disposal partner is key to maintaining sustainability and minimizing your environmental footprint.
[P] Miller Waste Systems is committed to providing hassle-free, sustainable waste management solutions for homeowners across Canada. By renting a bin, sorting your materials, and relying on us for responsible disposal, you can ensure that your yard work or renovation project is completed with minimal impact on the environment.

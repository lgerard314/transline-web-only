# Miller Environmental Corporation: A Research Report

## TL;DR
- Miller Environmental Corporation (MEC) is Manitoba's only fully licensed hazardous-waste treatment, storage and disposal operator, formed in 1995–96 as a 50/50 joint venture between the Province of Manitoba (through the Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation) and the Toronto-area Miller Group/Miller Paving, with its flagship Vaughn Bullough Environmental Centre near St. Jean Baptiste, Manitoba and its Winnipeg service centre at 1803 Hekla Avenue.
- The company is led by President Paul Bauer (since 2016, named in the 2026 WTC Winnipeg event coverage and on Canada's NPRI filings), with Blair McArthur signing as President & CEO at the corporate/parent level; long-time General Manager Vaughn Bullough — for whom the treatment facility was renamed in August 2022 — built the operation from 1997 onward.
- MEC has grown into one of Western Canada's largest hazardous-waste treatment platforms, is ISO 9001/14001/45001 certified, operates under Manitoba Licence No. 58 HW S2 RRRRR, and has no publicly reported environmental orders, fines, or community-opposition controversies on the public record — but it operates in a sensitive industry where reputational risk is significant and licence-modernization filings (most recently May 2025) are ongoing.

## Key Findings

**Corporate identity and ownership.** Miller Environmental Corporation is a 50/50 joint venture between the Province of Manitoba (via the Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation, a Crown entity created under the Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation Act, C.C.S.M. c. H15) and the privately-held Miller Group of Markham, Ontario (operating in Manitoba through its Miller Waste Systems / White Owl Group affiliates). The deal was announced on January 10, 1996 by then-Environment Minister Glen Cummings, with the Miller Group injecting private capital and management expertise into the previously wholly-public Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation. The structure remains in place today: Manitoba retains title to the land and leases it to MEC, while MEC operates the business and pays rent and dividends to the Crown shareholder. (Note: The Miller Group itself became a Colas Canada subsidiary on February 28, 2018, when Colas Canada completed its acquisition of 100% of the shares of the Miller McAsphalt Group for CAN $913 million per Colas SA's February 28, 2018 press release; MEC sits below that parent through the Miller Waste Systems / White Owl chain.)

**Founding and milestones.**
- **1986** – Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation Act enacted, creating the Crown corporation responsible for the province's hazardous-waste system.
- **1993** – Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation's St. Jean Baptiste / RM of Montcalm treatment facility receives its operating licence.
- **1995–96** – Miller Paving (parent of Miller Waste Systems) signs the 50/50 partnership with the Province to create the Miller Environmental Corporation operating entity, also running an interim bulk-storage operation at 55 Trottier Bay, Winnipeg under a 1995/1996 licence.
- **1997** – Vaughn Bullough, previously a Plant Manager at ChemSecurity Alta Ltd., is hired as General Manager and reorganizes operations.
- **2016** – Paul Bauer joins MEC and becomes President.
- **2019–2022** – Construction and licensing of Processing Cell 5 at the St. Jean Baptiste facility under Licence No. 58 HW S2 RRRR.
- **August 2022** – Manitoba Environmental Centre is renamed the **Vaughn Bullough Environmental Centre (VBEC)** in recognition of Bullough's 25-year leadership; announcement signed by Blair McArthur as President & CEO.
- **2023** – MEC joins the Mining Association of Manitoba Inc. as an Associate member.
- **October 2024 – May 2025** – Notice of Alteration filed and approved by Manitoba Environment and Climate Change to install a solvent-recycling system in Processing Building PB1, allowing in-province recycling of approximately 4.5 million litres of contaminated solvent received annually (previously fuel-blended and shipped to U.S. cement kilns). Licence updated to 58 HW S2 RRRRR.

**Services.** MEC is a "cradle-to-grave" hazardous-waste manager. Public service lines include: customer waste collection; 24/7 emergency response (spill containment and cleanup); environmental remediation (contaminated soil, brownfields, fire-damaged sites, UST excavation); industrial cleaning; industrial waste treatment (organic, inorganic, sludges, contaminated soils); project management; research & development; specialty recycling (solvent reclamation, precious-metal recovery, plastics shredding/washing); product stewardship; and vacuum-truck services. The company also holds a dedicated PCB-storage licence at the Winnipeg transfer centre and processes household hazardous waste (HHW) for Manitoba and Ontario.

**Facilities.**
- **Vaughn Bullough Environmental Centre (VBEC)** – Hwy 14 & 75, NE 2-3-1 EPM, RM of Montcalm, Manitoba R0G 2B0. Located ~70 km south of Winnipeg on a 64-hectare (~160-acre) footprint. Includes engineered-lined landfill (repository), clay-base lined processing cells, decontamination facility, hydro-vac slurring receiving area, process buildings for organic and inorganic waste treatment, and a plastics recycling facility.
- **Winnipeg Service Centre** – 1803 Hekla Avenue, Winnipeg, MB R2R 0K3. Hub for customer service technicians, project/field services and licensed PCB storage; coordinates inbound/outbound waste shipments. The public HHW drop-off at this address has been permanently closed; the City of Winnipeg now manages HHW through its 4R Depots.

**Certifications and Licences.** MEC operates under an Integrated Management System certified to **ISO 9001:2015 (Quality), ISO 14001:2015 (Environmental), and ISO 45001:2018 (Occupational Health & Safety)**. Key Manitoba regulatory licences include the VBEC Processing Facility Operating Licence **No. 58 HW S2 RRRRR** under the Dangerous Goods Handling & Transportation Act, and Hazardous Waste Transport Licence **MBC00202**. Federal hazardous-waste consignor/consignee registrations are MBG 02410 and MBR 01829. The company is also a profiled facility in the CHWMEG audit program used by Fortune-500 generators to vet disposal vendors.

**Leadership team.**
- **Paul Bauer** – President. 30+ years in environmental services; built and managed industrial landfills, hazardous-waste transfer facilities and soil-treatment sites across Canada; recipient of a lifetime achievement award from the Manitoba Environmental Industry Association (MEIA); University of Manitoba alumnus; based in Headingley, MB.
- **Blair McArthur** – President and CEO (signed the 2022 facility-renaming announcement); also Chairman of the White Owl Group, President & CEO of Miller Transit, and former SVP/COO of The Miller Group. McArthur's role appears to be at the parent/holding-company level.
- **Vaughn Bullough** – Vice President & General Manager (long-tenured; namesake of the treatment facility).
- **Dave Howes** – Director of Regulatory Affairs (named on Manitoba Environment filings).
- **Joel Carlson** – Domestic Sales & Marketing Representative.
- The company was publicly recruiting a VP Finance and an Executive Assistant via LinkedIn through 2024-2026.

**Notable projects (case studies on millerenvironmental.ca).**
- **Decommissioned grain elevator – Winnipeg.** Comprehensive remediation and demolition of a contaminated-registry grain elevator with widespread arsenic contamination, deteriorating water-filled basement and undocumented substructures. MEC chose full excavation/transport/treatment (vs. unproven in-situ arsenic stabilization), coordinating with Manitoba Hydro, City of Winnipeg Water & Waste and the Winnipeg Fire Department, with contaminated soil sent to VBEC for treatment.
- **Highway 16 diesel spill (November 15, 2024).** Saddle-tank rupture on a heavy truck released over 500 L of diesel into a roadside ditch near a CPKC railway line and a buried Bell MTS fibre cable. MEC removed ~28,000 L of diesel-contaminated water from a 45 m × 9 m area using hydro-vac soft-dig excavation, with PID monitoring, CCME compliance and a Remedial Action Plan filed with the province. Closed in 60 days for client Manitoba Public Insurance.
- **Steinbach strip mall fire-site hazard removal.** Post-fire asbestos abatement at a strip mall adjacent to residential housing and a children's playground; triggered a provincial stop-work order before MEC's revised abatement plan was approved; project closed in under 20 weeks, with asbestos transported in lined 80-yard bins and steel recycled.
- **Brandon power facility lime removal (March 2023).** Following a lime-silo rupture at a Brandon, Manitoba natural-gas power plant, lime powder spread across four of five floors with exothermic-reaction and respiratory hazards. MEC used HEPA-vacuum systems under negative-pressure containment, sealing lime in IBC containers, working with third-party industrial hygienist Pinchin Ltd. No safety incidents reported.

**Client industries.** Industrial manufacturing, aerospace, transportation, biotech, agriculture, mining, oil & gas, chemical distribution, utilities, the rail industry, Crown insurers (e.g., MPI), municipal household-hazardous-waste programs (including most Manitoba municipalities and select Ontario contracts), and federal/provincial agencies.

**Community involvement and affiliations.** MEC is a member of the Manitoba Environmental Industry Association (MEIA), Manitoba Chamber of Commerce, Canadian Manufacturers & Exporters (CME), Construction Safety Association of Manitoba, Manitoba Trucking Association, Winnipeg Chamber of Commerce, Ontario Waste Management Association (OWMA), the Mining Association of Manitoba Inc., and CODE (Commitment to Opportunity, Diversity and Equity). It is a **Proud Partner of the Winnipeg Blue Bombers** (CFL), sponsored the 2024 Annual Deputy Ministers' Dinner hosted by the Manitoba Chambers of Commerce, and operates a $1,000/year post-secondary education award program for dependents of employees, plus an internal Health and Safety Award (2025 recipient publicly recognized on LinkedIn).

**Sustainability.** Per President Paul Bauer at WTC Winnipeg's February 11, 2026 event: "We have in-house processes for all the waste we collect — 96 per cent is managed in-house and four per cent gets sent out to other hazardous waste management facilities" (wtcwinnipeg.com, March 15, 2026). The 2024–25 solvent-recycling installation is expected to convert roughly 4.5 million litres per year of contaminated solvent from a fuel-blending (U.S. cement-kiln) end-use into reclaimed solvents for the paints-and-coatings industry, materially raising the company's recovery rate. MEC self-describes its model as "All roads lead back to Miller" and pitches itself as among Canada's most environmentally sustainable and respected hazardous-waste treatment and disposal facilities.

**Controversies/issues.** No public enforcement orders, fines, or significant odour/spill complaints tied to the VBEC facility were located in searches of Manitoba's environmental public registry, gov.mb.ca news, or major Manitoba media outlets (Winnipeg Free Press, CBC, Winnipeg Sun, La Liberté). The 1995 Trottier Bay (Winnipeg) interim-bulk-storage licence contains a standard "odour nuisance" clause (triggered by five-plus complaints from unrelated residents) but no public record of those complaints being substantiated. Earlier (pre-Miller) Clean Environment Commission siting hearings (1987 and 1992) for the Montcalm facility were part of standard public consultation, not a controversy. The Steinbach project's temporary provincial stop-work order is the only documented regulatory friction and was resolved through a revised abatement plan.

## Details

### History and the public-private model
Manitoba was an early Canadian adopter of a dedicated provincial hazardous-waste system. After the 1986 enabling Act and the 1993 licensing of the Montcalm treatment facility, the Province concluded that the operation needed private capital and a sharper operating culture. The 1996 partnership with Miller Paving (the predecessor of today's Miller Group, headquartered in Markham, Ontario and — since Colas Canada's February 28, 2018 acquisition of 100% of the shares of the Miller McAsphalt Group for CAN $913 million — a Colas Canada subsidiary) gave the province a private partner without giving up sovereignty over the land or the underlying regulatory framework. The Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation (MHWMC) remains a Crown corporation that leases the St. Jean Baptiste land to MEC and collects rent and dividends, while MEC handles operations.

That structure is unusual in Canada and helps explain MEC's market position: as a Crown-anchored, privately-operated facility, it is the **only fully licensed hazardous-waste treatment and disposal facility of its kind in Manitoba** and one of only two such facilities in Canada per President Paul Bauer's February 2026 remarks at WTC Winnipeg. The franchise quality has translated into steady scope expansion — Processing Cell 5 (constructed 2019–22), solvent recycling (approved 2025), and ongoing licence modernization filings.

### Vaughn Bullough's legacy
Bullough — recruited by Miller in 1997 — built the operation from a struggling Crown-asset takeover into what MEC now describes as "one of Canada's most environmentally sustainable, diverse and respected hazardous waste treatment and disposal facilities." The 2022 renaming of the flagship facility in his honour is exceptional in Manitoba's environmental-services sector and signals strong continuity of leadership culture even as the day-to-day President's chair has transitioned to Paul Bauer.

### Paul Bauer's strategy
At WTC Winnipeg's February 11, 2026 event at Shrugging Doctor Beverage Company in Winnipeg, Bauer described a strategy built on three legs: (1) maximize in-house processing — "96 per cent is managed in-house and four per cent gets sent out to other hazardous waste management facilities" — to capture margin and minimize regulatory exposure from third-party shipments; (2) reach beyond Manitoba's small home market — MEC now accepts hazardous waste from across Canada and the U.S.; and (3) preserve relationships in a tight market ("In this small market, Winnipeg and Manitoba, do not burn a bridge"). His 2,000+ remediation-project background and MEIA Lifetime Achievement Award give the operating company credibility with both regulators and large industrial generators.

### Regulatory posture
The May 2, 2025 Notice of Alteration approval (Licence 58 HW S2 RRRRR) shows a constructive, ongoing relationship with Manitoba's Environmental Approvals Branch: MEC files modernization requests, the Branch responds with conditions, and a public Citizens Liaison Committee (CLC) reviews proposals. The recently approved solvent recycling unit (an Almatec AC-150 system installed in Processing Building PB1) is the most significant operational change in recent years and shifts MEC further up the waste hierarchy from fuel-blending to true material recovery.

### Independent corporate family
- **Miller Environmental Corporation, Winnipeg/RM of Montcalm, MB** – the subject of this report; hazardous waste; 50% Province of Manitoba, 50% Miller Group/White Owl (Miller Group being a Colas Canada subsidiary since February 28, 2018).
- **Miller Environmental Group Inc. (MEG), Calverton, NY** – an unrelated, U.S.-based environmental response company founded by Jim Miller Sr. in 1971 (Exxon Valdez responder, U.S. Coast Guard OSRO). As of September 10, 2024, MEG is owned by Coalesce Capital, a New York-based private equity firm, which acquired it from GenNx360 Capital Partners (Houlihan Lokey deal announcement and PR Newswire release, September 11, 2024). **No corporate relationship to the Canadian Miller Environmental Corporation.**
- **Miller Environmental, Inc., Anaheim, CA** – a separate 1999-founded California demolition/abatement contractor. **Not related to the Canadian company.**

This naming overlap is a common source of confusion when researching MEC.

## Recommendations

**For prospective clients (industrial generators or municipalities seeking Manitoba/Western-Canada hazardous-waste services):** MEC is effectively the default Manitoba operator with the broadest service envelope in the province. Verify scope coverage of the specific waste codes you generate against Licence 58 HW S2 RRRRR (publicly available on gov.mb.ca), ask explicitly about the new solvent-recycling pathway if your waste stream contains contaminated solvents (better recovery profile than cement-kiln fuel-blending), and request the company's current CHWMEG audit profile — most large generators with formal vendor-vetting programs will already have access.

**For prospective employees / job seekers:** MEC is hiring in technical, sales and finance roles in Winnipeg. Salary + benefits + the $1,000/year post-secondary award and the ISO-45001-aligned safety culture compare favourably to the broader industry. Strongest fit for chemists, engineers-in-training, vacuum-truck operators, and Class 1A drivers.

**For investors / M&A analysts:** The 50/50 Crown-private structure is durable but means MEC is not a freely tradable asset. The investible exposure is at the **Miller Group / White Owl Group** parent level (privately held; Colas Canada is the corporate parent of Miller Group via the 2018 transaction). Track signals worth watching: (a) any change to the Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation Act that would alter the Crown stake; (b) successful commissioning of the solvent-recycling system and its margin uplift; (c) any further licence modernizations (Processing Cell 6 would be a key tell); and (d) any provincial-policy push to enforce stricter in-province disposal of hazardous waste, which would expand MEC's addressable market.

**For Manitoba community stakeholders (RM of Montcalm, St. Jean Baptiste, environmental NGOs):** The Citizens Liaison Committee structure and the public registry mean filings are accessible — use the gov.mb.ca registry file 3440.20 to monitor alterations. The 2024–25 solvent-recycling installation is a positive sustainability move, but new on-site chemistry should be tracked. The fact that there are no publicly reported orders or community complaints on file is a meaningful baseline; any future change would be a noteworthy signal.

**Decision thresholds that should change these recommendations:**
- A material environmental incident at VBEC, a Director's enforcement order, or substantiated odour complaints under Licence 58 HW S2 RRRRR → re-evaluate counter-party risk and reputational exposure.
- A change to the 50/50 Crown-private ownership (full Crown buy-out or full Miller Group buy-out) → reset on the structural-moat thesis.
- Loss or non-renewal of any ISO certification → re-evaluate as a vendor for ISO-mandated clients.

## Caveats

- **Public-record search limits.** Major Manitoba dailies (Winnipeg Free Press, La Liberté, Winnipeg Sun) are partly behind paywalls; absence of negative coverage in the searches conducted is suggestive but not absolute. CBC Manitoba and gov.mb.ca news did not surface enforcement actions.
- **Leadership reconciliation.** MEC's "About Us" page still carries Blair McArthur's signature as "President and CEO" from the 2022 facility-renaming announcement, while the 2026 WTC Winnipeg article and 2023 NPRI filing list Paul Bauer as President. The most defensible interpretation is that McArthur holds the President & CEO title at the corporate/parent-group level (consistent with his concurrent Miller Transit and White Owl Group roles) while Bauer is the President of the operating company day-to-day. The website may simply not have been updated to reflect a separation of titles.
- **Grain-elevator project specifics.** The MEC case study does not name the specific Winnipeg grain elevator or give a completion year; readers wanting that level of detail will need to contact the company directly.
- **Financial data.** As a private joint venture, MEC does not publish revenue or profit numbers. Dun & Bradstreet and ZoomInfo carry estimates only (employee count ~45–74 FTE per NPRI 2023 / third-party directories). The Manitoba Hazardous Waste Management Corporation's audited statements (gov.mb.ca, 2023) provide partial insight into the Crown side of the joint venture but not into operating-company financials.
- **"Miller Environmental" naming.** Be careful not to confuse the Canadian MEC with the unrelated U.S. firms Miller Environmental Group Inc. (Calverton, NY; owned by Coalesce Capital since September 10, 2024) and Miller Environmental, Inc. (Anaheim, CA), which have separate ownership, history and operations.
